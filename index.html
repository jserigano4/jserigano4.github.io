About Me

I am a data scientist with a background in astronomy, physics, and planetary science. I use my experience with large data sets in combination with Python, SQL, MATLAB, and various machine learning techniques to explore data, understand trends, predict future outcomes, and provide tangible results. I am interested in all aspects of data science and I aspire to leverage my prior experiences as a scientist to produce creative solutions to environmental and societal issues.

I earned my Ph.D. in Earth and Planetary Sciences from Johns Hopkins University where I focused on atmospheric data analysis and modeling. My thesis explored the compositional interactions between Saturn’s upper atmosphere and ring system using data from the Ion and Neutral Mass Spectrometer aboard NASA’s Cassini spacecraft. I also hold a B.A. in astronomy and physics from Boston University and have worked as a research scientist at NASA Goddard Spaceflight Center and CERN.

After receiving my Ph.D., I joined General Assembly’s Data Science Immersive course to grow my technical skills and expand my skill set before transitioning from a career in academia to industry. This intensive program allowed me to develop a deeper understanding of many skills pertinent to a career in data science, including Python, SQL, Pandas, machine learning, statistical methods, predictive modeling toolkits like scikit-learn, and many other techniques.

In my free time I enjoy running, traveling the globe, cooking delicious food, pottery, and spending time with my rescue dog, Woody.
